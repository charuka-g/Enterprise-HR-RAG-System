MERIDIAN HOLDINGS PLC
DRESS CODE AND PROFESSIONAL APPEARANCE POLICY
Policy Code: HR-LK-011
Effective Date: 1 January 2025
Last Reviewed: October 2024
Approved By: Group Head of Human Resources

==========================================================================
1. PURPOSE
==========================================================================

This policy sets out the professional appearance and dress code standards for employees of Meridian Holdings PLC in Sri Lanka. A professional appearance reflects well on the Company, builds client confidence, and contributes to a respectful workplace environment.

The Company recognizes cultural, religious, and individual diversity and has aimed to make this policy inclusive and practical.

==========================================================================
2. SCOPE
==========================================================================

This policy applies to all employees of Meridian Holdings PLC and its subsidiaries in Sri Lanka while on Company premises, at client sites, at external business meetings, or representing the Company in any capacity. It applies regardless of whether the employee is working in the office, meeting a client, or attending a Company event.

==========================================================================
3. GENERAL STANDARDS
==========================================================================

All employees are expected to present themselves in a manner that:
  - Is clean, neat, and well-maintained
  - Is appropriate for a professional corporate environment
  - Does not cause offence or discomfort to colleagues, clients, or visitors
  - Reflects the Company's values of professionalism and integrity

Personal hygiene is a component of professional appearance. Employees are expected to maintain appropriate standards of personal cleanliness.

==========================================================================
4. DRESS CODE BY BUSINESS UNIT
==========================================================================

4.1 Head Office and Corporate Functions (HR, Finance, Legal, Strategy)

Standard: Business Formal

  Men:
    - Formal trousers, business shirt, and tie (client-facing days) OR
    - Smart trousers with an open-collar formal shirt (non-client days)
    - Formal shoes
    - Jacket/blazer for client meetings and senior management interactions

  Women:
    - Formal trouser suit, skirt suit, or formal dress
    - Smart formal blouse with trousers or skirt
    - Formal closed-toe shoes recommended; clean open-toe sandals acceptable
    - Saree is always appropriate and encouraged

  Smart Casual Friday:
    - Every Friday is Smart Casual day. Neat jeans (no rips), smart tops, and casual shoes are acceptable.

4.2 Meridian Tech (IT)

Standard: Smart Casual (daily)

  - Clean, well-fitting casual wear including neat jeans, chinos, casual shirts, polo shirts
  - Branded company T-shirts are encouraged
  - Trainer / sneakers acceptable if clean and in good condition

  Client-facing roles in Tech (e.g., project managers, sales engineers):
  - Business casual required on client visit days (formal trousers, smart shirt)

4.3 Meridian Retail

Standard: Company Uniform

  - All customer-facing Retail employees are provided with a Company uniform
  - Uniforms must be worn every working day, be kept clean and pressed, and properly fitted
  - Employees bear the cost of replacement uniforms if lost or damaged through negligence
  - Grooming standards: Hair should be neat and tied back where required by store safety guidelines; visible tattoos should be covered where practicable; excessive jewelry is not permitted

4.4 Meridian Manufacturing

Standard: Protective Work Wear

  - Safety boots, helmets, high-visibility vests, and other Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) as prescribed by the Safety Officer are mandatory on the manufacturing floor
  - No loose clothing, open-toed shoes, or exposed jewelry on the factory floor
  - Overalls or Company-issued workwear must be worn as directed

==========================================================================
5. CULTURAL AND RELIGIOUS DRESS
==========================================================================

Meridian Holdings PLC fully respects the right of employees to wear religious or culturally significant attire, including but not limited to:
  - Hijab, niqab, or other Islamic modest dress
  - Sikh turban or dastar
  - Hindu religious markings (pottu, vibhuti)
  - Buddhist robes (for employees who are ordained on approved religious leave days)
  - Sarees and traditional Sri Lankan national dress

No employee will be disadvantaged, criticized, or asked to remove legitimate religious or cultural dress. Any concern about religious dress raised by a colleague or manager should be immediately referred to the HR Business Partner.

==========================================================================
6. EXCEPTIONS AND SPECIAL OCCASIONS
==========================================================================

6.1 Dress-Down Events

On designated Company events (e.g., Family Day, Annual Sports Day, Charity Walk Days), employees may be notified of a more relaxed or themed dress code. These will be communicated in advance.

6.2 Remote Working

Employees working from home are expected to maintain appropriate professional appearance for video calls. Smart casual attire is the minimum expectation on video. Formal meetings (with clients or senior leadership) require business casual as a minimum.

6.3 Pregnancy

Pregnant employees are not expected to conform to standard dress norms where it would be impractical or uncomfortable to do so. Comfortable, neat, and appropriate maternity wear is fully acceptable.

==========================================================================
7. WHAT IS NOT ACCEPTABLE
==========================================================================

The following are not acceptable in the workplace regardless of BU:
  - Torn, stained, excessively worn, or ill-fitting clothing
  - Clothing with offensive, political, or controversial slogans
  - Clothing that exposes undergarments
  - Overly revealing clothing that may be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
  - Footwear inappropriate for the workplace (e.g., flip-flops in corporate offices)
  - Visible excessive body piercings (facial piercings beyond a single nose stud in corporate environments)

==========================================================================
8. MANAGER RESPONSIBILITIES
==========================================================================

Managers are responsible for addressing dress code concerns within their team sensitively and privately. Any conversation about an employee's appearance must be handled respectfully and without embarrassment to the employee. Managers should consult their HR Business Partner if unsure how to raise a concern.

==========================================================================
9. NON-COMPLIANCE
==========================================================================

Employees who do not comply with this policy may be asked to go home and change before returning to work. Repeat non-compliance will be addressed under the Disciplinary Policy. Employees will not face pay deductions for the time taken to change, on a first occasion.
