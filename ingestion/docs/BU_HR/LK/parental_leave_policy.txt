MERIDIAN HOLDINGS PLC
MATERNITY, PATERNITY AND PARENTAL LEAVE POLICY
Policy Code: HR-LK-003
Effective Date: 1 January 2025
Last Reviewed: October 2024
Approved By: Group Head of Human Resources

==========================================================================
1. PURPOSE
==========================================================================

Meridian Holdings PLC is committed to supporting employees through significant life events, including the birth, adoption, or legal guardianship of a child. This policy sets out the entitlements and procedures for maternity leave, paternity leave, and adoption leave in line with the Maternity Benefits Ordinance (Cap. 187) of Sri Lanka and the Company's commitment to being a family-friendly employer.

==========================================================================
2. SCOPE
==========================================================================

This policy applies to all permanent employees of Meridian Holdings PLC and its Sri Lankan subsidiaries. Contract employees should refer to their individual contracts, though the Company will endeavor to apply the same spirit of this policy where feasible.

==========================================================================
3. MATERNITY LEAVE
==========================================================================

3.1 Entitlement

All permanent female employees are entitled to maternity leave as follows:

  First and Second Child:
  - Duration: 84 calendar days (12 weeks)
  - Pay: Full basic salary and fixed allowances for the entire duration

  Third Child and Beyond:
  - Duration: 84 calendar days (12 weeks)
  - Pay: As per the Maternity Benefits Ordinance (14 days at full pay; remainder unpaid unless otherwise agreed)

Note: The Company voluntarily pays full salary for the first and second child as an enhanced benefit above the statutory minimum.

3.2 Commencement of Maternity Leave

Maternity leave may commence:
  - Up to 4 weeks before the expected date of delivery, or
  - On the day of delivery

Employees are encouraged to plan their leave start date in consultation with their doctor and line manager.

3.3 Extension of Maternity Leave

Employees may extend maternity leave beyond 84 days using:
  - Annual leave balance (paid)
  - Unpaid leave (maximum 3 additional months, with BU Head and HR Director approval)

3.4 Notification Requirements

Employees should notify their line manager and HR Business Partner of their pregnancy as soon as practicable. Formal notification of intended maternity leave dates must be given at least 6 weeks before the planned start date, along with a medical certificate indicating the expected date of delivery.

3.5 Ante-Natal Appointments

Pregnant employees are entitled to paid time off to attend ante-natal medical appointments. Employees must provide advance notice and evidence of the appointment where possible.

3.6 Health and Safety During Pregnancy

The Company will conduct a risk assessment for all pregnant employees and make reasonable adjustments to working arrangements where necessary, including:
  - Adjustment of duties to avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activity
  - Permission to work from home where the role allows
  - Allocation of a suitable rest area

3.7 Return to Work

Upon return from maternity leave, the employee is entitled to return to the same role and grade she held before the leave, on the same terms and conditions. If this is not practicable due to genuine business reasons, the Company will offer a suitable alternative role of equivalent grade and salary.

Nursing mothers are entitled to two 30-minute feeding breaks per working day for a period of 6 months following return to work. The Company will provide a private, hygienic space for this purpose.

==========================================================================
4. PATERNITY LEAVE
==========================================================================

4.1 Entitlement

All permanent male employees are entitled to paternity leave of 10 working days with full pay on the occasion of the birth of their child. This applies to biological fathers and legally recognized partners.

4.2 Timing

Paternity leave must be taken within 4 weeks of the birth of the child. It may be taken as a block or split into two separate periods, subject to manager agreement.

4.3 Additional Paternity Leave

Employees may apply to take additional unpaid leave or annual leave beyond the 10-day entitlement to support their partner or care for the newborn.

4.4 Notification

Employees should notify their manager and HR as early as possible, and no later than 2 weeks before the expected birth date where known, or immediately upon the birth.

==========================================================================
5. ADOPTION LEAVE
==========================================================================

5.1 Entitlement

Employees who adopt a child under the age of 5 are entitled to adoption leave as follows:
  - Primary caregiver (regardless of gender): 84 calendar days at full pay (first adoption), 42 calendar days (second adoption)
  - Secondary caregiver / partner: 10 working days at full pay

5.2 Eligibility

The employee must provide documentation from the relevant court or adoption authority confirming the adoption. Leave commences from the date the child is placed in the employee's care.

==========================================================================
6. PREGNANCY LOSS AND FERTILITY TREATMENT
==========================================================================

6.1 Pregnancy Loss

The Company recognizes the physical and emotional impact of pregnancy loss (including miscarriage, stillbirth, and neonatal loss). Employees who experience pregnancy loss are entitled to:
  - Up to 10 days of paid compassionate leave for loss occurring at 12 weeks or later
  - Up to 5 days of paid compassionate leave for loss occurring before 12 weeks
  - Access to the Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) for counselling

Partners of employees who experience pregnancy loss are entitled to 3 days of paid compassionate leave.

6.2 Fertility Treatment

Employees undergoing fertility treatment (IVF or other assisted reproduction) are entitled to up to 3 days per treatment cycle of paid time off for medical appointments, subject to relevant medical documentation. This is in addition to standard medical leave entitlements.

==========================================================================
7. KEEPING IN TOUCH (KIT) DAYS
==========================================================================

An employee on any form of parental leave may voluntarily work up to 10 Keeping in Touch (KIT) days during their leave period without this affecting their leave entitlement or pay. KIT days must be mutually agreed upon by the employee and their manager. KIT days are paid at the employee's normal daily rate.

==========================================================================
8. BENEFITS DURING PARENTAL LEAVE
==========================================================================

During all forms of paid parental leave, all contractual benefits (including health insurance, EPF/ETF contributions, and annual leave accrual) continue as normal. During unpaid extended leave, health insurance coverage continues but EPF/ETF contributions are not made.

==========================================================================
9. NON-DISCRIMINATION
==========================================================================

Meridian Holdings PLC prohibits any discrimination, demotion, disadvantaging, or negative treatment of an employee because of pregnancy, maternity leave, or any other form of parental leave. Any employee who believes they have been treated unfairly should raise the matter through the Grievance Policy (HR-LK-006).
