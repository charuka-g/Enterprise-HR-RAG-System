MERIDIAN HOLDINGS PLC
GRIEVANCE HANDLING POLICY
Policy Code: HR-LK-006
Effective Date: 1 January 2025
Last Reviewed: October 2024
Approved By: Group Head of Human Resources

==========================================================================
1. PURPOSE
==========================================================================

Meridian Holdings PLC is committed to maintaining a fair, respectful, and productive workplace. This policy provides a clear and structured process for employees to raise concerns, complaints, or grievances in a safe and confidential manner. The Company will treat all grievances seriously and resolve them promptly and fairly.

A grievance is any concern, problem, or complaint that an employee wishes to raise about their work, working conditions, relationships at work, or how they have been treated by a colleague, manager, or the Company.

==========================================================================
2. SCOPE
==========================================================================

This policy applies to all current employees of Meridian Holdings PLC, including permanent staff, probationary employees, and fixed-term contract employees. It covers grievances related to (but not limited to):
  - Terms and conditions of employment
  - Workplace relationships, including conflict with a colleague or manager
  - Discrimination, harassment, or bullying
  - Health, safety, and working environment concerns
  - Application of Company policies
  - Perceived unfair treatment

==========================================================================
3. GUIDING PRINCIPLES
==========================================================================

  - All employees have the right to raise a grievance without fear of retaliation.
  - Grievances will be treated confidentially to the extent possible.
  - Grievances will be investigated impartially and by individuals with no personal stake in the outcome.
  - The aim is to resolve grievances at the lowest possible level and as quickly as possible.
  - Raising a grievance in good faith will not have any negative consequences for the employee.
  - Deliberate false or malicious allegations will be treated as a disciplinary matter.

==========================================================================
4. INFORMAL RESOLUTION (STAGE 1)
==========================================================================

4.1 In most cases, an employee should first try to resolve the matter informally by:
  - Speaking directly with the person involved (where safe and appropriate to do so), or
  - Raising the matter with their direct line manager

4.2 The line manager should acknowledge the concern within 2 working days and attempt to facilitate an informal resolution within 5 working days.

4.3 A record of the informal discussion should be kept on file by the manager (not on the employee's permanent HR record).

4.4 Where the grievance is against the line manager, or where the employee is uncomfortable raising it with the line manager, they may escalate directly to Stage 2.

==========================================================================
5. FORMAL GRIEVANCE (STAGE 2)
==========================================================================

5.1 Submitting a Formal Grievance

If informal resolution is unsuccessful or inappropriate, the employee may submit a formal written grievance to the HR Business Partner. The grievance should be submitted using the Grievance Submission Form available on the ESS portal or in writing addressed to the HR Business Partner.

The written grievance should include:
  - The nature of the grievance in detail
  - Relevant dates, incidents, and persons involved
  - What outcome the employee is seeking
  - Any evidence the employee wishes to submit

5.2 Acknowledgement

The HR Business Partner will acknowledge receipt of the formal grievance within 2 working days and appoint a Grievance Investigator (typically an HR Business Partner or senior manager from another department with no involvement in the issue).

5.3 Formal Grievance Meeting

The Grievance Investigator will invite the employee to a formal meeting within 5 working days of acknowledgement. The employee has the right to be accompanied to this meeting by:
  - A colleague of their choice (not a legal representative), or
  - A recognized trade union representative

The role of the companion is to support the employee, not to speak on their behalf.

5.4 Investigation

Following the meeting, the Grievance Investigator will:
  - Speak with relevant parties (including the respondent, if applicable)
  - Review any evidence submitted
  - Consider previous related incidents or patterns

5.5 Outcome

The employee will receive a written outcome within 10 working days of the formal grievance meeting. The outcome will include:
  - A summary of findings
  - The decision
  - Any actions the Company will take
  - Information on the right to appeal

==========================================================================
6. APPEAL (STAGE 3)
==========================================================================

6.1 If the employee is not satisfied with the formal grievance outcome, they may appeal in writing within 5 working days of receiving the outcome. The appeal must state the specific grounds for appeal (e.g., procedural error, new evidence, or the decision being unreasonable).

6.2 The appeal will be heard by a senior leader who was not involved in the original investigation. For grievances involving Grade M3 and above, the appeal will be heard by the Group HR Director.

6.3 The appeal outcome is final. The employee will receive a written decision within 10 working days of the appeal meeting.

==========================================================================
7. GRIEVANCES INVOLVING HARASSMENT OR DISCRIMINATION
==========================================================================

Grievances alleging harassment (including sexual harassment), bullying, or discrimination based on gender, ethnicity, religion, disability, age, marital status, or any other protected characteristic will be handled under this policy with heightened sensitivity and confidentiality. Additional steps may include:
  - Separation of the employee from the alleged perpetrator pending investigation
  - Involvement of an external investigator where warranted
  - Reference to the Meridian Anti-Harassment Policy (HR-GLOBAL-003)

==========================================================================
8. WHISTLEBLOWING
==========================================================================

Employees wishing to report concerns about fraud, financial misconduct, illegal activity, or serious regulatory violations should use the Meridian Whistleblower Hotline (1-800-MERIDIAN-WB) or email: whistleblower@meridianholdings.lk. Whistleblowing concerns are governed by a separate policy and are handled with full anonymity where desired.

==========================================================================
9. CONFIDENTIALITY AND RECORDS
==========================================================================

All grievance records, including written submissions, investigation notes, and outcomes, are stored securely by HR and are accessible only on a need-to-know basis. Grievance records are retained for 7 years. Records of unsubstantiated grievances will not form part of the respondent's employment record.
