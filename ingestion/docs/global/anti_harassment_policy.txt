MERIDIAN HOLDINGS PLC
ANTI-HARASSMENT, ANTI-BULLYING AND NON-DISCRIMINATION POLICY
Policy Code: HR-GLOBAL-003
Effective Date: 1 January 2025
Last Reviewed: October 2024
Approved By: Group CEO and Group Chief Human Resources Officer

==========================================================================
1. COMMITMENT STATEMENT
==========================================================================

Meridian Holdings PLC is unconditionally committed to providing a workplace where every employee is treated with dignity, fairness, and respect. We will not tolerate harassment, bullying, or discrimination in any form — whether it occurs in the office, at work events, on business travel, or through digital channels such as email and messaging apps.

This policy applies to the conduct of all employees regardless of grade or seniority. Senior employees who harass or bully those in their team will be subject to more serious consequences, not less, due to the power differential involved.

==========================================================================
2. SCOPE
==========================================================================

This policy applies to:
  - All employees, contractors, interns, and directors of Meridian Holdings PLC globally
  - Conduct occurring during working hours, at Company premises, at Company-sponsored events, or in any context connected to employment (including social media in a work-related context)

==========================================================================
3. DEFINITIONS
==========================================================================

3.1 Harassment

Harassment is any unwanted conduct that has the purpose or effect of:
  - Violating a person's dignity, or
  - Creating an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating, or offensive environment

Harassment may be based on (but is not limited to):
  - Gender / sex
  - Race, ethnicity, or national origin
  - Religion or belief
  - Age
  - Disability
  - Sexual orientation
  - Gender identity or expression
  - Marital or family status
  - Pregnancy or maternity

Examples of harassment include:
  - Offensive jokes, slurs, or derogatory comments about a person's identity
  - Displaying or sharing offensive images, memes, or materials
  - Mimicking or mocking a person's accent, disability, or cultural practices
  - Persistent unwanted social invitations or contact after being asked to stop
  - Deliberate exclusion of a person from work activities based on their identity

3.2 Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment is a form of harassment that includes:
  - Unwelcome physical contact (touching, hugging, kissing without consent)
  - Sexual comments, innuendo, or jokes
  - Sending explicit or sexual messages, images, or materials
  - Persistent unwanted romantic or sexual attention
  - Requesting sexual favors in exchange for work benefits, promotions, or continued employment (quid pro quo)
  - Creating an environment where sexual humor or objectification is normalized

Sexual harassment may be committed by anyone of any gender toward anyone of any gender.

3.3 Bullying

Bullying is persistent, offensive, intimidating, malicious, or insulting behavior that undermines an individual. Examples include:
  - Persistent criticism or humiliation in front of colleagues
  - Deliberately setting impossible workloads or unrealistic deadlines to cause failure
  - Excluding or isolating a person from the team without legitimate reason
  - Withholding necessary information to sabotage a person's performance
  - Undermining someone's work or ideas to damage their confidence or reputation
  - Shouting or aggressive behavior in the workplace

Note: Constructive feedback, performance management, and reasonable instructions — even when difficult — do not constitute bullying.

3.4 Discrimination

Unlawful discrimination occurs when an employee is treated less favorably (direct discrimination) or is disadvantaged by a neutral policy (indirect discrimination) based on a protected characteristic (see 3.1 above).

==========================================================================
4. RESPONSIBILITIES
==========================================================================

4.1 All Employees

  - Treat every colleague with respect at all times
  - Challenge inappropriate behavior when safe to do so
  - Report concerns through the channels in Section 7

4.2 Managers and Leaders

  - Model inclusive and respectful behavior
  - Address concerns raised by their team members promptly and sensitively
  - Never dismiss, minimize, or retaliate against an employee who raises a concern
  - Proactively create an environment where everyone feels safe to speak up

4.3 HR Business Partners

  - Support employees and managers in understanding and applying this policy
  - Conduct or commission fair and confidential investigations into complaints
  - Track trends and patterns and report concerns to senior leadership

==========================================================================
5. WHAT THIS POLICY IS NOT
==========================================================================

This policy is not intended to prevent:
  - Legitimate and constructive performance feedback
  - Reasonable managerial instruction or direction
  - Healthy debate and disagreement on work matters
  - Robust but respectful challenge of ideas

Disagreement with a decision or a critical performance review does not in itself constitute harassment or bullying.

==========================================================================
6. IMPACT OF HARASSMENT ON THE COMPLAINANT
==========================================================================

Meridian Holdings PLC recognizes that harassment and bullying can have a serious and lasting impact on the victim's mental health, confidence, and career. The Company commits to:
  - Taking all complaints seriously, regardless of the seniority of the alleged perpetrator
  - Providing emotional support through the Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)
  - Ensuring the complainant is not disadvantaged in any way for raising a concern

==========================================================================
7. REPORTING A CONCERN
==========================================================================

Employees who experience or witness harassment, bullying, or discrimination should report it through one of the following channels:

  a) Speak to your HR Business Partner directly
  b) Submit a formal grievance via the ESS portal (Grievance Policy HR-LK-006)
  c) Use the Meridian Whistleblower Hotline (anonymous option available):
     Phone: 1-800-MERIDIAN-WB
     Email: whistleblower@meridianholdings.lk
  d) Speak to any member of the Meridian Anti-Harassment Champions Network (list available on the HR portal)

Employees are encouraged to report concerns as early as possible. Historical concerns can still be raised and will be investigated sensitively.

==========================================================================
8. INVESTIGATION PROCESS
==========================================================================

8.1 All complaints will be acknowledged within 2 working days.

8.2 An impartial investigator (HR Business Partner or external investigator where required) will be appointed. The investigator will have no personal connection to either party.

8.3 Both the complainant and respondent will have the opportunity to present their account and evidence.

8.4 The investigation will be conducted as confidentially as possible. Only individuals who need to know will be informed.

8.5 Interim protective measures (e.g., temporary separation of parties, remote working) may be put in place during the investigation to protect the complainant.

8.6 The investigation outcome will be communicated in writing to both parties, along with information on any actions being taken and the right to appeal.

==========================================================================
9. CONFIDENTIALITY
==========================================================================

Confidentiality will be maintained throughout the investigation to the greatest extent possible. However, absolute confidentiality cannot always be guaranteed where the investigation requires interviewing witnesses or where there is a statutory obligation to disclose.

==========================================================================
10. CONSEQUENCES
==========================================================================

Substantiated findings of harassment, bullying, or discrimination will result in disciplinary action up to and including immediate termination of employment, regardless of the perpetrator's seniority, tenure, or contribution to the business.

In serious cases (particularly criminal sexual assault or sustained victimization), the matter will be referred to law enforcement authorities.

==========================================================================
11. NON-RETALIATION
==========================================================================

Retaliation against any employee for raising a complaint in good faith, participating in an investigation, or supporting a colleague in making a complaint is strictly prohibited and is itself a disciplinary offence. Employees who experience retaliation should report it immediately to the HR Director or via the Whistleblower channel.
