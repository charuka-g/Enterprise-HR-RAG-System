MERIDIAN HOLDINGS PLC
CODE OF CONDUCT AND BUSINESS ETHICS
Policy Code: HR-GLOBAL-001
Effective Date: 1 January 2025
Last Reviewed: October 2024
Approved By: Group Chief Executive Officer

==========================================================================
1. INTRODUCTION
==========================================================================

Meridian Holdings PLC is one of Sri Lanka's largest conglomerates, with over 14,000 employees across Finance, Retail, IT, and Manufacturing business units. Our reputation is built on trust, integrity, and the consistent ethical conduct of every employee and representative.

This Code of Conduct sets out the standards of behavior expected from all employees, officers, directors, contractors, and business partners acting on behalf of Meridian Holdings PLC. It is not an exhaustive set of rules, but a framework for making sound ethical decisions in the course of your work.

When in doubt, ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my actions were reported on the front page of a national newspaper?" If the answer is no, reconsider the action.

==========================================================================
2. SCOPE
==========================================================================

This Code applies to all full-time employees, part-time employees, contractors, interns, and directors of Meridian Holdings PLC and all its subsidiaries globally, without exception, regardless of seniority.

==========================================================================
3. INTEGRITY AND HONESTY
==========================================================================

3.1 Employees must act honestly and with integrity in all dealings — with colleagues, clients, suppliers, regulators, and the public.

3.2 Falsification of records, misrepresentation of facts, or dishonest conduct in any business dealing is a serious disciplinary offence that may result in immediate termination and legal action.

3.3 Employees must never misrepresent their qualifications, experience, or the capabilities of Company products and services.

==========================================================================
4. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
==========================================================================

4.1 Employees must avoid situations where their personal interests conflict (or appear to conflict) with the interests of Meridian Holdings PLC.

4.2 A conflict of interest may arise when:
  - An employee or their close family member has a financial interest in a supplier, client, or competitor
  - An employee engages in outside employment or consulting that competes with or affects their work for the Company
  - An employee makes or influences business decisions for personal financial gain

4.3 All actual or potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed to the employee's line manager and HR Business Partner in writing using the Conflict of Interest Declaration Form on the ESS portal. Disclosure does not automatically mean the conflict is prohibited; in many cases it can be managed appropriately.

==========================================================================
5. GIFTS, HOSPITALITY AND ANTI-BRIBERY
==========================================================================

5.1 Meridian Holdings PLC has a zero-tolerance policy on bribery and corruption in all its forms, whether direct or indirect.

5.2 Employees may accept modest, non-cash gifts from business partners or clients (e.g., branded merchandise, food hampers at festive seasons) valued below LKR 5,000. Gifts above this value must be reported to HR and either returned or donated to a Company-designated charity.

5.3 Cash gifts (in any amount) and gifts that could influence a business decision must never be accepted or offered.

5.4 Business hospitality (meals, events, sports tickets) is acceptable when it is:
  - Proportionate and not lavish
  - Not given or received in connection with a current contract negotiation or tender process
  - Properly recorded in the Meridian Hospitality Register (managed by Finance)

5.5 Any demand or inducement for a payment or benefit in exchange for awarding a contract, expediting a process, or obtaining a regulatory approval must be reported immediately to the Whistleblower Hotline.

==========================================================================
6. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION
==========================================================================

6.1 Employees have access to sensitive Company information as part of their roles. This includes financial data, client lists, pricing, strategic plans, employee records, and intellectual property. This information is confidential and must not be shared with unauthorized individuals inside or outside the Company.

6.2 Confidentiality obligations survive the termination of employment.

6.3 Personal data of employees, clients, and other individuals must be handled in accordance with the Meridian Data Privacy Policy and applicable data protection laws.

6.4 Employees must not use personal USB drives or unauthorized cloud storage (e.g., personal Google Drive, Dropbox) to store or transfer Company data.

==========================================================================
7. RESPECT AND INCLUSION IN THE WORKPLACE
==========================================================================

7.1 Meridian Holdings PLC is committed to providing a workplace free from discrimination, harassment, and bullying. Every employee deserves to be treated with dignity and respect.

7.2 Discrimination on the basis of gender, age, ethnicity, religion, nationality, disability, marital status, pregnancy, political opinion, or sexual orientation will not be tolerated.

7.3 Harassment, including sexual harassment, verbal abuse, intimidation, or behavior that demeans, threatens, or humiliates another person, is strictly prohibited and will result in serious disciplinary action, including termination.

7.4 Employees who witness or experience harassment or discrimination are encouraged to report it under the Grievance Policy (HR-LK-006) or the Whistleblower Policy.

==========================================================================
8. USE OF COMPANY ASSETS AND SYSTEMS
==========================================================================

8.1 Company assets — including laptops, mobile phones, vehicles, office equipment, and intellectual property — are provided for business use. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work performance or violate any other policy provision.

8.2 Company IT systems must not be used to access, store, or distribute:
  - Illegal, offensive, or discriminatory content
  - Pirated software or media
  - Malware or unauthorized applications

8.3 Employees are responsible for the safekeeping of Company assets assigned to them. Lost or damaged assets must be reported to IT/Administration immediately.

==========================================================================
9. SOCIAL MEDIA AND EXTERNAL COMMUNICATIONS
==========================================================================

9.1 Employees are personally responsible for what they post on personal social media accounts. Content that:
  - Disparages or brings Meridian Holdings PLC into disrepute
  - Discloses confidential Company information
  - Harasses or demeans a colleague, client, or competitor
  - Is defamatory, offensive, or illegal
...is prohibited and may result in disciplinary action.

9.2 Only designated spokespersons (typically the Communications department or C-suite) are authorized to speak to the media on behalf of Meridian Holdings PLC. All media enquiries must be referred to Group Communications.

==========================================================================
10. HEALTH, SAFETY AND ENVIRONMENT
==========================================================================

10.1 Every employee has a duty to maintain a safe working environment. Unsafe conditions, accidents, or near-misses must be reported to the Safety Officer immediately.

10.2 Employees must not attend work under the influence of alcohol or illegal substances. Violation of this rule is a serious disciplinary offence.

10.3 Meridian Holdings PLC is committed to environmental sustainability. Employees are encouraged to minimize waste, reduce energy consumption, and adopt responsible practices in their daily work.

==========================================================================
11. COMPLIANCE WITH LAWS AND REGULATIONS
==========================================================================

11.1 Employees must comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and Company policies in the performance of their duties.

11.2 Any activity that constitutes a breach of applicable law — including tax evasion, money laundering, insider trading, or false accounting — will result in immediate termination and referral to the relevant authorities.

==========================================================================
12. REPORTING CONCERNS
==========================================================================

If you become aware of or suspect a violation of this Code of Conduct or any Company policy, you are obligated to report it through one of the following channels:
  - Your line manager or HR Business Partner
  - The Meridian Whistleblower Hotline: 1-800-MERIDIAN-WB
  - Email: whistleblower@meridianholdings.lk

Retaliation against any employee who reports a concern in good faith is strictly prohibited and is itself a disciplinary offence.

==========================================================================
13. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION
==========================================================================

Violations of this Code will be taken seriously and addressed through the Meridian Disciplinary Process. Depending on the severity, consequences may include:
  - Formal warning (written)
  - Suspension
  - Termination of employment
  - Civil or criminal legal proceedings

==========================================================================
14. ACKNOWLEDGEMENT
==========================================================================

All employees are required to read and electronically acknowledge this Code of Conduct upon joining and annually thereafter via the ESS portal. Acknowledgement confirms that the employee understands and agrees to comply with these standards.

For queries, contact hr@meridianholdings.lk or speak with your HR Business Partner.
